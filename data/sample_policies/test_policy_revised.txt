NATIONAL EMPLOYMENT POLICY 2024 - REVISED INCLUSIVE DRAFT

Section 1: Definitions

1.1 For the purpose of this Act, "worker" means every person employed to do skilled, semi-skilled, or unskilled manual, technical, operational, clerical, supervisory, contract, platform, or gig work. Every worker shall be entitled to the rights set forth herein without discrimination.

1.2 The term "employer" refers to any person, enterprise, or public authority that engages workers in any industry or service.

Section 2: Employment Rights

2.1 Every worker shall be entitled to a minimum wage published through transparent rules, written criteria, and periodic review. Any deviation from the published standard must be recorded with reasons and may be challenged through an appeal process.

2.2 Eligible workers shall receive parental, caregiving, and medical leave on equal and non-discriminatory terms, without distinction based on gender or marital status.

2.3 Benefits under this scheme shall be available to permanent, contract, and gig workers. Any eligibility threshold must be objectively justified, proportionate, and accompanied by a written right to review.

Section 3: Social Security

3.1 The government may implement this Act only in accordance with published rules, public consultation, and independent oversight. No class of workers may be excluded without written justification and a review mechanism.

3.2 Workers in urban, rural, and small-town areas shall receive an equal baseline package of benefits. Additional support may be provided only where evidence shows higher need or cost.

3.3 Old age pension shall be available through flexible service requirements that account for disability, illness, caregiving interruptions, and other documented barriers to continuous service.

Section 4: Grievance Redressal

4.1 Any worker aggrieved by a decision may approach the Labour Commissioner and shall receive written reasons for the decision. The worker shall have a right to appeal to an independent authority within a reasonable time.

4.2 Any amendment, modification, or repeal of this Act shall be preceded by public notice, consultation with affected workers, and publication of the final reasons for change.
