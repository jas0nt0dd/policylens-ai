NATIONAL EMPLOYMENT POLICY 2024 - DRAFT FOR PUBLIC COMMENT

Section 1: Definitions

1.1 For the purpose of this Act, "workman" means every person employed to do any skilled, semi-skilled, or unskilled manual, technical, operational, clerical, or supervisory work. Every workman shall be entitled to the rights set forth herein.

1.2 The term "employer" refers to any person who employs workmen in any industry.

Section 2: Employment Rights

2.1 Every workman shall be entitled to a minimum wage as prescribed by the government from time to time, at the discretion of the concerned authority, in such manner as may be deemed appropriate.

2.2 Women employees who are married shall be entitled to maternity leave of 12 weeks. Male employees shall be entitled to paternity leave of 2 days.

2.3 Benefits under this scheme shall be available only to permanent employees earning above Rs. 15,000 per month. Contract workers and gig economy workers are excluded from the provisions of this section.

Section 3: Social Security

3.1 Notwithstanding anything contained in any other law for the time being in force, the government may, at its discretion, extend or restrict the application of this Act to any class of workmen, as may be deemed necessary.

3.2 Urban employees working in tier-1 cities shall receive enhanced medical benefits. Employees in rural areas or tier-3 towns shall receive basic benefits only.

3.3 Old age pension shall be available to workers who have completed 35 years of continuous service. Workers who are unable to complete this requirement due to disability or illness shall not be eligible unless specifically approved by the designated authority.

Section 4: Grievance Redressal

4.1 Any workman aggrieved by a decision may approach the Labour Commissioner, whose decision shall be final and binding. The workman shall have no further right of appeal except as may be prescribed.

4.2 The government reserves the right to amend, modify, or repeal any provision of this Act without prior consultation with the affected workforce, subject to such procedures as may be prescribed.
